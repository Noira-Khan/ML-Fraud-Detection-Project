	
    
    The Problem: Credit card fraud costs the company millions (direct financial loss)
    and ruins customer trust.

	The Stakeholder Requirement: Build a system that catches fraudulent transactions 
    in real-time (to save money/increase ROI) BUT it must not aggressively block normal customers.
    If an innocent user's card is declined at a restaurant because our AI falsely flagged it,
    that UX cost leads to customer churn.

    The Solution: Build a predictive Machine Learning model that evaluates a transaction 
    at the moment of swipe and flags it as safe or suspicious.

    This is a Binary Classification problem.
	The target Variable (Y) is the Class column in the dataset.
	    Y=1 (The transaction is Fraudulent).
        Y=0 (The transaction is Legitimate).

    Success Metrics (KPIs)
	Why Accuracy is a Lie: In fraud detection, 99.8% of transactions are legitimate. 
    If I build a "dumb" model that simply predicts 0 (Legitimate) every single time, 
    my model will be 99.8% accurate, but it will catch exactly zero fraud.
	My Accurate KPIs:
		Recall (Sensitivity): Out of all the actual fraud that occurred, 
        what percentage did I catch? (Crucial for stopping financial loss).

		Precision: Out of all the transactions I flagged as fraud,
        how many were actually fraud? (Crucial for not annoying legitimate customers).

        F1 Score : Mean of Precision and Recall


        AUPRC (Area Under the Precision-Recall Curve): This is the ultimate metric that I will use
        to grade AI model.
